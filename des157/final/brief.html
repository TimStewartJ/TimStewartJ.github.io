Project Brief

Project Summary

For the final project, I hope to expand on the every picture studio through more interaction and expanded visuals.

Comparison 1

This New York Times article captures a lot of what I want to accomplish for the final. Scrolling through the site, the gun is taken apart and its insides are revealed. I want to offer that same level of exposure for my PC and how that is revealed. One thing to note is that the site seems to use a video for the graphics. Because it scrubs through the video as the user scrolls, it gives off a choppy effect. A picture of the site is shown below.

Comparison 2

This datacenter tour site from Microsoft also embodies a bit of what I want to accomplish. This interactive experience allows the user to go through and explore their data center, roughly similar to what I want my users to experience when going through my PC. Microsoft's site does take a bit to load and is fairly hard to run since it involves high-quality 3D visualizations. While that would be cool to implement for my project, it isn't feasible and I'll likely be sticking to the two dimensional format. Another picture is shown below.

Project Goals

My specific goal is to implement more moving parts. Right now, the site is awfully static. Similar to the New York Times article, I want for there be a sense of depth and technicality.

Design Strategies

I don't have too much in mind for changing the actual underlying design. I'm quite content with the font and color choice and how that all comes together. I might try to add more sleek-ness by eliminating round corners and bezels, but that is not something I've decided yet.

Interaction Strategies

I've decided that this will be my main area of focus for the project. My initial thoughts were to manipulate the images I have even more. Perhaps I'll layer the elements of the PC further and incorporate a parallax effect. I would also like to have the specific parts clickable to pull up more details and information on them.

Experience Goals

With this project, I want the audience to see what example is inside of the magic boxes that we use every day for nearly everything. I also hope to simplify what's inside of a computer for the audience and show that it isn't necessarily a super complex thing.
